1  Introduction

1.1 Introduction to Change Management and Organizational Agility Workshops

Welcome to our series of workshops focusing on Change Management and Organizational Agility. In today’s fast-paced business environment, the ability to adapt to change and foster agility within organizations is crucial for success. These workshops aim to equip participants with the knowledge, skills, and strategies needed to navigate change effectively and cultivate a culture of agility within their organizations.

Workshop 1: “Introduction to Change Management and Organizational Agility” In this workshop, participants will gain a foundational understanding of change management principles and the importance of organizational agility. They will learn about key concepts, best practices, and frameworks that can help them effectively lead and manage change initiatives within their organizations.

Workshop 2: “Developing Dynamic Capabilities for Change and Agility” Building upon the fundamentals covered in the first workshop, this session will delve deeper into developing dynamic capabilities that enable organizations to adapt quickly to changing environments. Participants will explore strategies for building resilience, fostering innovation, and creating a culture that embraces continuous improvement and learning.

Join us on this journey of discovery and empowerment as we explore the transformative power of change management and organizational agility. Get ready to enhance your skills, drive innovation, and lead your organization towards a more agile and resilient future.

From: “Business Model Evolution Using the Portfolio Map” (n.d.)

Change Management stands as the bedrock of our series, characterized by systematic methodologies to manage organizational transitions. We delve into traditional change management strategies, highlighting the importance of structured planning and execution to ensure stability and predictability. This section establishes a solid foundation for understanding the disciplined nature of change management.

Shifting gears, we explore Organizational Agility, which signifies a more fluid and responsive approach to change, transcending the linear confines of traditional change management. Agility focuses on rapid adaptation to change with minimal upheaval, underscoring the significance of speed, flexibility, and perpetual learning. This segment demonstrates how agility can be infused into the corporate ethos, extending beyond the realm of IT to become a core strategic advantage.

1.2 Learning Outcomes

By participating in this series, attendees will gain:

  1. A Dual Perspective on Change Management: Insights into the interplay between traditional change management and organizational agility, highlighting how they complement and contrast with each other.
  2. Agile Transformation Insights: An understanding of how agility can be seamlessly integrated with structured change management to strike a balance between stability and adaptability.
  3. Holistic Leadership for Change: Skills to navigate and drive change using a combined approach of traditional and agile methodologies, suitable for their organizational context.
  4. Strategic and Practical Applications: Through case studies and interactive sessions, participants will apply these concepts to real-world scenarios, enabling comprehensive change, enhancing agility, and cultivating innovation in their teams and organizations.

This series aims to challenge conventional wisdom, urging participants to critically analyze the facets of change management, and agility, and their integrated application for achieving strategic and operational excellence.

1.3 Programme

Here’s a detailed program outline for the two workshops on Change Management and Organizational Agility, including learning outcomes and key components for each session:

1.3.1 Workshop 1: Introduction to Change Management and Organizational Agility

1.3.1.1 Session 1: Change Management

  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Key Components:
    • Overview of Change Management: Definition, importance, and evolution over time.
    • Change Management Models: Review of major models like Lewin’s Change Model, Kotter’s 8-Step Process, and the ADKAR model.
    • Role of Leaders: How leadership is pivotal in driving and sustaining change.
    • Change Resistance: Understanding the psychology of change resistance and strategies to overcome it.
  • Learning Outcomes:
    • Grasp the fundamental concepts and importance of change management.
    • Understand various change management models and their practical applications.
    • Recognize the critical role of leadership in facilitating change.
    • Develop strategies to manage resistance to change effectively.

1.3.1.2 Session 2: Organizational Agility

  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Key Components:
    • Agility in Organizations: Definition and significance in the modern business landscape.
    • Agile Methodologies: Exploration of methodologies like Scrum, Kanban, and Lean.
    • Building an Agile Culture: Key elements of an agile organization and how to foster an agile culture.
    • Case Studies: Examination of successful agile transformations in various industries.
  • Learning Outcomes:
    • Understand the concept of organizational agility and its business value.
    • Learn about different agile methodologies and their application in non-IT contexts.
    • Identify the key factors that contribute to building and sustaining an agile culture.
    • Analyze real-world examples of agile transformations to extract best practices and lessons learned.

1.3.2 Workshop 2: Developing Dynamic Capabilities for Change and Agility

1.3.2.1 Session 1: Measuring Dynamic Capabilities

  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Key Components:
    • Concept of Dynamic Capabilities: Definition and relevance in change management and agility.
    • Assessing Capabilities: Tools and frameworks for assessing an organization’s dynamic capabilities.
    • Benchmarking and Analytics: Techniques for measuring and comparing capabilities against industry standards.
    • Feedback Loops: Establishing systems to monitor, learn, and adapt capabilities continuously.
  • Learning Outcomes:
    • Understand the concept of dynamic capabilities and their impact on organizational change and agility.
    • Learn how to assess and measure the dynamic capabilities within an organization.
    • Utilize benchmarking and analytics to inform strategic decisions and improvements.
    • Implement feedback loops to ensure continuous learning and capability development.

1.3.2.2 Session 2: Developing Dynamic Capabilities

  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Key Components:
    • Strategic Development: Strategies for developing dynamic capabilities to enhance change management and agility.
    • Change Leadership: Skills and strategies for leaders to foster an environment conducive to developing dynamic capabilities.
    • Innovation and Learning: Integrating continuous learning and innovation into the development of dynamic capabilities.
    • Action Planning: Creating a roadmap for developing and implementing dynamic capabilities in participants’ organizations.
  • Learning Outcomes:
    • Develop strategies to enhance an organization’s dynamic capabilities for better change management and agility.
    • Understand the role of leadership in cultivating an environment that supports dynamic capability development.
    • Integrate continuous learning and innovation processes into the organizational fabric to boost dynamic capabilities.
    • Formulate an action plan to develop and implement dynamic capabilities in the workplace.

This program is designed to provide a comprehensive understanding of change management and organizational agility, focusing on both foundational knowledge and practical skills for developing dynamic capabilities.
